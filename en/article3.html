Radioactive: The Film That Brings Marie Curie’s Story Back to Life

How well do you really know Marie Curie?

By Leila Tebrouri

4 min read.

Marie Curie won two Nobel Prizes and was a pioneer in both physics and chemistry, but most people only know her as a picture in a textbook. The movie Radioactive (2019), directed by Marjane Satrapi, changes that by showing the real Marie Curie:a brilliant scientist but also a strong, emotional, and very human woman.

A biography that feels personal

Released in 2019 and starring Rosamund Pike, Radioactive follows Marie Curie from her first experiments to her biggest discoveries about radioactivity. Instead of being a typical biopic, the movie focuses on her personal journey. It shows her challenges, her moments of doubt, and how hard it was to be a woman in a field dominated by men. We see a passionate and determined Marie Curie who keeps fighting to have her work recognized, even when people underestimate her.

A woman in a man’s world

At the end of the 19th century, women were rarely accepted in science. Even when they made major discoveries, their work was often ignored or credited to men. The film shows this reality very clearly. When Marie Curie wins her first Nobel Prize, the media mostly talks about her husband,Pierre Curie. And after he dies, she still has to fight to be recognized for her own research. Her story also reminds us of Nettie Stevens, another scientist whose work was overshadowed by a man.

A movie that looks beautiful and feels powerful

Marjane Satrapi created a movie that is both visually stunning and full of emotion. The story switches between Curie’s discoveries and the later consequences of radioactivity, from Hiroshima to Chernobyl. This mix shows how science can be both amazing and dangerous. The soundtrack, lighting, and smooth transitions between time periods make the movie feel intense and moving.

A tribute to women in science

More than just telling Marie Curie’s story, Radioactive celebrates all the women who contributed to science but were never fully recognized. It reminds us that curiosity, hard work, and courage have nothing to do with gender, only with passion. That’s also what Nettieverse is trying to do: give a voice to the women history forgot.
