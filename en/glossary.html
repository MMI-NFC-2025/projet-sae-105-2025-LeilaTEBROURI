Glossary

  1. Chromosome: Rod-shaped structure found in the nucleus of cells, carrying the genes responsible for heredity.
  2. Genetics: Science that studies genes, heredity, and the transmission of biological traits.
  3. X Chromosome / Y Chromosome: The chromosomes that determine the biological sex of an individual (XX for female, XY for male).
  4. Matilda Effect: Concept describing the tendency to minimize or attribute to men the discoveries made by women scientists.
  5. Invisibilization: Process by which the contribution or presence of women is ignored or erased in historical or scientific accounts.
  6. Sexism: Attitude or discriminatory behavior based on sex, often to the detriment of women.
  7. Scientific Recognition: Having one's work and discoveries officially recognized by the scientific community.
  8. Nuclear Fission: Chemical reaction in which the nucleus of an atom splits, releasing a large amount of energy.
  9. Radioactivity: Natural phenomenon by which certain elements spontaneously emit radiation.
  10. Noether's Theorem: Fundamental principle linking physical symmetries to conservation laws in physics.
  11. X-ray Diffraction: Method used to determine the structure of molecules, notably DNA.
  12. Double Helix (DNA): Two-stranded spiral shape that describes the structure of the DNA molecule.
  13. International Federation of University Women (IFUW): Organization founded in 1919 to promote the place of women in research and higher education.
  14. Research Grants: Financial aid awarded to researchers to support their work.
  15. Gender History: Discipline that studies the social construction of feminine and masculine identities over time.
  16. Gender Stereotypes: Preconceived ideas and generalizations about behaviors or roles attributed to women and men.
  17. Equality in Science: Principle aimed at offering women and men the same opportunities in scientific careers.
  18. Representation of Women: The way women are presented or perceived in media, literature, or science.
  19. Scientific Legitimacy: Recognition of a person's competence and credibility in the scientific field.
  20. Rehabilitation: Action of restoring the place and recognition of forgotten figures.
  21. Pioneer: Woman who paves the way in a new or difficult field.
  22. Nobel Prize: International distinction rewarding the greatest discoveries in various fields, including physics, chemistry, and medicine.
  23. Laboratory: Place equipped for conducting research and scientific experiments.
  24. Scientific Research: Methodical approach aimed at acquiring new knowledge or verifying a hypothesis.
  25. Biography: Story of a person's life, often famous or inspiring.
