The Matildas Timeline

Discover the stories of amazing women scientists who helped shape our world, even if history didn't always remember them.

Matilda Joslyn Gage (1826–1898)

She was one of the first to speak out about how women were erased from the history of science. Sadly, her ideas were overshadowed by more famous suffragettes like Susan B. Anthony.

Nettie Stevens (1861–1912)

She discovered that biological sex is determined by X and Y chromosomes. But the credit usually went to Thomas Hunt Morgan.

Lise Meitner (1878–1968)

She co-discovered nuclear fission with Otto Hahn, but he was the only one to get the Nobel Prize in Chemistry.

Emmy Noether (1882–1935)

She created Noether's Theorem, a cornerstone in modern physics, but her groundbreaking work was ignored for years and misled by male colleagues.

Cecilia Payne-Gaposchkin (1900–1979)

She proved that stars are mostly made of hydrogen and helium, but her discovery was first credited to another astronomer, Norris Russell.

Rosalind Franklin (1920–1958)

Her X-ray photos of DNA made it possible to discover its double-helix structure, yet the recognition went to Watson, Crick, and Wilkins instead.

Jocelyn Bell Burnell (born 1943)

She discovered pulsars during her PhD, but when the Nobel Prize came, it went to her supervisor, Antony Hewish, who received the Nobel Prize.

Margaret W. Rossiter (born 1944)

She coined the term "Matilda Effect" to describe how women's scientific achievements are often credited to male colleagues. Her own research has sometimes been overlooked too.

Grace Hopper (1906–1992)

She developed the first modern programming language, which became the foundation of COBOL. At the time, her breakthrough wasn't made superiors.

Hedy Lamarr (1914–2000)

Along with George Antheil, she invented a frequency-hopping system that later inspired Wi-Fi and Bluetooth technology. Her invention was ignored for decades.

Katalin Karikó (born 1955)

She played a major role in developing mRNA vaccines like the COVID-19 vaccine, but her initial work wasn't recognized by big pharma committee, showing that the Matilda effect is not recognized by the Nobel.

⚠️ Not every Matilda is represented in this timeline, but each one deserves to be remembered.
