Films, Series, and Books About the Matilda Effect

Welcome to the gallery!

Films :

Les Figures de l’ombre (Hidden Figures)

Ce film raconte l’histoire vraie de trois mathématiciennes afro-américaines qui ont travaillé à la NASA dans les années 1960. Grâce à leurs calculs, les États-Unis ont pu envoyer des astronautes dans l’espace, mais leurs noms ont longtemps été oubliés. Si vous préférez lire, il existe aussi le livre du même nom écrit par Margot Lee Shetterly.

Voir le film

Marie Curie : The Courage of Knowledge

Raconte la vie de Marie Curie après la mort de son mari. Le film montre ses recherches sur la radioactivité et les difficultés qu’elle a rencontrées pour faire reconnaître son travail dans un milieu scientifique dominé par les hommes.

Voir le film

Amelia Earhart

The film tells the story of Amelia Earhart, the first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic. Even though it's about aviation, it shows how women have to fight for recognition in dominated fields.

Watch the movie

Contact

This movie follows an astronomer who discovers an alien signal and must convince male scientists to take her seriously.

Watch the movie

Series :

Call the Midwife

A series about midwives in London during the 1950s. They do essential work needed in society but often goes unrecognized.

Watch the series

The Astronaut Wives Club

This series tells the story of the wives of American astronauts in the 1960s, and the crucial support they provided during space missions.

Watch the series

Books :

Rise of the Rocket Girls by Nathalia Holt

Tells the story of the women "computers" of NASA in the 1950s and 1960s, who helped in the space race but remained in the shadows.

See the book

Invisible Women by Caroline Criado Perez

Explains how women are still often invisible in many areas today, not just in science.

See the book

The Madame Curie Complex by Julie Des Jardins

It analyzes the life of Marie Curie and other women scientists whose accomplishments have been overlooked too.

See the book
